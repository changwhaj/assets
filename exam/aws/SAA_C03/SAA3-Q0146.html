The reasons are: On-Demand Instances provide stable, reliable baseline capacity for the normal workload. Spot Instances can provide the additional capacity needed during peak periods at a much lower hourly rate compared to On-Demand. The stateless nature of the application allows taking advantage of Spot without affecting availability. If Spot is interrupted, the baseline On-Demand capacity remains available. Reserved Instances require upfront commitment and may not match the variable workload. Dedicated Instances are more expensive than On-Demand for baseline capacity. Using only Spot Instances risks interruption during peak times if capacity is not available.
