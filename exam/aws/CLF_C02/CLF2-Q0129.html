Selected Answer: A
For an uninterruptible workload that runs once a year just for 24 hours, the most cost-efficient Amazon EC2 pricing model would be On-Demand Instances. On-Demand Instances are ideal for short-term workloads that spike periodically or unpredictable workloads that can’t be interrupted. Reserved Instances are best suited for workloads that run continuously for a year or longer, while Spot Instances are most cost-effective for workloads that can tolerate downtime and have flexible start and end times. Dedicated Instances are not a pricing model, but rather a physical Amazon EC2 server dedicated to a single customer.
