This one is hard. I think it could be A, B and C. if the current workload demands for NoSQL capabilities are better suited for DynamoDB and the existing RDS resources are underutilized, migrating to DynamoDB can be a form of rightsizing. This migration aligns the resources more closely with the actual needs of the workload, potentially resulting in cost savings compared to maintaining underutilized RDS instances. B is more an up front decision in the design phase, so A and C may be better answers here.
