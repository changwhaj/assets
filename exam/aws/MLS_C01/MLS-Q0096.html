Answer A. One of the key assumptions of linear regression is that the residuals have constant variance at every level of the predictor variable(s). If this assumption is not met, the residuals are said to suffer from heteroscedasticity. When this occurs, the estimates for the model coefficients become unreliable https://www.statology.org/constant-variance-assumption/
