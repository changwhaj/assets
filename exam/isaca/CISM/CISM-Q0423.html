Configuration management Configuration management is a process that proactively minimizes the likelihood of disruption, unauthorized alterations, and errors during the implementation of a new system. This process involves identifying, controlling, maintaining, and verifying the configuration items (CIs) of a system, such as software, hardware, and documentation. By managing the configuration of a system, it is possible to ensure that changes to the system are made in a controlled and authorized manner, that the integrity of the system is maintained, and that the system is able to function as intended. Additionally, Configuration management also includes monitoring and reporting on the configuration of a system,
