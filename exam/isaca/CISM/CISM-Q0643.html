A redundant power supply is when a single piece of computer equipment operates using two or more physical power supplies. Each of the power supplies will have the capacity to run the device on its own, which will allow it to operate even if one goes down. For normal operation, each of the power supplies will provide half (assuming there are two) of the power that is needed. If one is powered off for some reason, the other one will immediately compensate to provide full power to the device so there is no downtime at all.
