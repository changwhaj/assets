A newly hired information security manager discovers that the cleanup of accounts for terminated employees happens only once a year. Which of the following should be the information security manager’s FIRST course of action?

  • A. Design and document a new process.
  • B. Perform a risk assessment.
  • C. Report the issue to senior management.
  • D. Update the security policy.

Selected Answer: B
Risk Assessment: Performing a risk assessment is crucial in understanding the potential security risks and vulnerabilities associated with the current practice of cleaning up accounts for terminated employees only once a year. This step allows the manager to identify and quantify the risks associated with this practice, including the possibility of unauthorized access, data breaches, and compliance violations. While reporting the issue to senior management (Option C) is important, it should be done after conducting a risk assessment. Having concrete data on the risks involved will strengthen the manager's case when discussing the issue with senior management and seeking their support for changes.
