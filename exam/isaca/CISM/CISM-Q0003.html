The answer is “D”. The reason "B. conduct a risk assessment" isn't the FIRST step is because a risk assessment evaluates the potential risks associated with identified vulnerabilities or gaps. Before you can assess risks, you need to know where the deficiencies or gaps exist in relation to the new regulatory requirement. Performing a gap analysis first allows you to identify where your organization's current controls or practices differ from what the new regulation mandates. Only after identifying these gaps can you effectively assess the risks associated with not addressing them. In simpler terms, you can't assess the risks of a gap if you haven't identified that gap exists in the first place. Hence, performing a gap analysis is the initial step before a risk assessment in the context of new regulatory requirements.
