Selected Answer: A
Establishing key risk indicators allows the organization to define and monitor specific metrics that reflect its risk appetite. KRIs provide a quantitative or qualitative measurement of risk and help in assessing whether the risk treatment options align with the organization's desired risk tolerance. By defining and monitoring KRIs, the organization can track risk levels, identify potential deviations from the desired risk appetite, and take appropriate actions to address any significant variances. This enables a proactive approach to risk management and ensures that risk treatment decisions are consistent with the organization's risk appetite.
