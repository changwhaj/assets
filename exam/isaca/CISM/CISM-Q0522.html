A. are mapped to core strategic initiatives Key risk indicators (KRIs) are metrics that are used to measure the likelihood and potential impact of specific risks to an organization. They are most effective when they are mapped to the organization's core strategic initiatives, as this allows the organization to understand how the identified risks may impact its ability to achieve its goals. This information can then be used to prioritize risk management efforts and to make informed decisions about how to mitigate or manage those risks. While comparison with industry peers, redefining the KRIs on a regular basis, or assessing progress toward declared goals can also provide valuable insights, but these are secondary compared to the first one.

They are often used to identify and flag potential problems before they become actual risks.
