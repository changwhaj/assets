The primary reason an information security manager should restrict the use of generic administrator accounts in a multi-user environment is to ensure that segregation of duties is maintained. In a multi-user environment, it is important to ensure that no single user has too much control or access to sensitive information. By restricting the use of generic administrator accounts, organizations can ensure that access to sensitive information and critical systems is limited to only those who need it, and that multiple people are involved in any critical activities, preventing a single person from having too much control.
