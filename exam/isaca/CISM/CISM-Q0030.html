Selected Answer: B
Defining the issues to be addressed is the first step in developing a business case for a new intrusion detection system (IDS) solution because it allows the information security manager to clearly understand and communicate the specific problems that the organization is facing with its current security controls. Once the issues have been identified, the information security manager can then evaluate different solutions to determine which one is the best fit to address those issues. This will help in creating a more effective and compelling case for the new IDS solution, it also allows the manager to demonstrate the potential benefits and cost savings of the new solution to the organization, and helps in gaining the support of stakeholders.
