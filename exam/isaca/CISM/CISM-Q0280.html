C. Parallel testing Parallel testing is the most effective way to allow for disaster recovery testing without interrupting business operations. In parallel testing, the organization runs a copy of its production systems in parallel with the live systems. This allows the organization to test its disaster recovery processes and procedures without disrupting the normal operations of the business. The organization can test its disaster recovery procedures in a controlled environment and identify any issues or gaps in its plans. By doing this, it can improve its disaster recovery capabilities and ensure that it is able to quickly and effectively recover its systems in the event of a disaster.

C. Parallel testing is the most effective way to allow for disaster recovery testing without interrupting business operations. Parallel testing involves running the primary and backup systems in parallel, with the primary system handling the live production workload and the backup system running a copy of the same workload in a simulated environment. This approach ensures that the backup system is up to date and fully functional, without disrupting the primary system's operations.
