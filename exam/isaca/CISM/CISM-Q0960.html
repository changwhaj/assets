Selected Answer: B
B. verify to recipients the integrity of an email message. An email digital signature is a cryptographic technique that is used to verify the integrity of an email message. It ensures that the content of the email has not been altered during transmission or tampered with in any way. The digital signature is generated using a private key that is unique to the sender and can only be decrypted using the corresponding public key. When the recipient receives the email, they can verify the digital signature using the sender's public key, which confirms that the email has not been modified since it was signed by the sender. This helps to ensure the authenticity and integrity of the email message.
