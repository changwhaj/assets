A daily monitoring report reveals that an IT employee made a change to a firewall rule outside of the change control process. The information security manager's FIRST step in addressing the issue should be to:

  • A. perform an analysis of the change.
  • B. report the event to senior management.
  • C. require that the change be reversed.
  • D. review the change management process.

Selected Answer: D
D. review the change management process. Before taking any immediate actions like analyzing the change, reporting to senior management, or requiring the change to be reversed, it's important to understand why the change control process was bypassed in the first place. Reviewing the change management process will help identify any gaps, weaknesses, or reasons why employees might be circumventing it. This step is crucial to prevent similar incidents in the future and ensure that the change control process is effective. Once the process has been reviewed and potentially improved, further actions can be taken based on the findings.
