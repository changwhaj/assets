Promoting the information security mission within the enterprise (option A) goes beyond just developing procedures. It focuses on creating awareness, engaging employees, and fostering a security-conscious mindset. By promoting the mission, organizations can encourage employees to actively participate in security initiatives, take ownership of information security, and understand the role they play in protecting sensitive data. This approach can lead to a more sustainable and effective change in security culture and behavior.

Selected Answer: A
A. Promoting the information security mission within the enterprise is the MOST effective way to change the security culture and behavior of staff. When staff members understand the importance of information security and are committed to the mission, they are more likely to engage in security best practices and report security incidents. Promotion of information security can be done through security awareness campaigns, regular training sessions, and other communication initiatives. Enforcement and auditing are important components of an information security program, but they are not as effective in changing the culture and behavior of staff as promoting the security mission.
