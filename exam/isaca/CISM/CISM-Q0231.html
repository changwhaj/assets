The first thing that the information security manager should do when developing materials to update the board, regulatory agencies, and the media about a security incident is to invoke the organization's incident response plan. The incident response plan is a pre-established set of procedures and processes that are put in place to help organizations respond quickly and effectively to security incidents. It should be activated as soon as an incident is detected, in order to ensure that the incident is contained, investigated and resolved in an efficient and effective manner. Once the incident response plan is invoked, the information security manager should then determine the needs and requirements of each audience. This includes understanding the specific information that each audience needs in order to make informed decisions, and tailoring the communication to meet those needs.
