Selected Answer: D
The information security manager should contact the information owner as soon as possible after the "incident has been confirmed". Confirming the incident typically involves an initial assessment to determine the scope and impact of the incident, and to determine whether or not it is indeed a security breach. Once the incident has been confirmed, the information security manager should immediately contact the information owner to inform them of the situation and to coordinate a response. This is important because the information owner is responsible for the sensitive information that may have been impacted and will need to assess the potential risk to the organization and its customers or stakeholders. It is also important to note that contacting the information owner early in the incident response process helps to ensure that the right individuals are involved in the response and can help to minimize the damage caused by the incident.
