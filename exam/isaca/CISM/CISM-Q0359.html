The correct answer is (A) Business continuity plan [BCP] as that is the only one that has the proper contact information. Rationale: (A) a BCP has all the things required to respond to a disaster this includes the following: - Executive summary - Business impact analysis - Recovery strategies - Communication plan - Testing and maintenance (B) An SLA measures the quality of the service and has the following components in it: - Description of services - Service levels - Measurements - Reporting - Remedies - Termination As you can see there is no contact information (C) A Security charter talks about intent so this doesn't work either (D) Business Impact Analysis is a decomposition of critical processes using the following: - Identification of critical business functions and process - assessment of the impact of disruption - quantification of the impact - prioritization of critical business functions and processes - development of recovery strategies.

Selected Answer: B
An SLA is a contract between a service provider and a customer that outlines the level of service that will be provided. In the case of equipment and software vendors, the SLA would specify the level of support and maintenance that the vendor will provide to the customer. In order to ensure that the customer can quickly and easily contact the vendor for support and maintenance, the SLA should include contact information for the vendor's representatives. This might include phone numbers, email addresses, and/or physical addresses where the vendor's representatives can be reached. Including this information in the SLA helps to ensure that any issues or problems with the equipment or software can be quickly addressed, minimizing downtime and disruptions to the customer's business.
