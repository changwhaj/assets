Selected Answer: C
C. Backups are maintained offline and regularly tested. Ransomware attacks can encrypt or compromise data, making it inaccessible. Having offline backups that are regularly tested ensures that even if the primary data is compromised, the organization can recover its data from a secure and unaffected source. This is a critical element in mitigating the impact of a ransomware attack and ensuring business continuity. While options A, B, and D are important security measures, they are not as crucial as having offline backups in the context of a ransomware attack.
