Selected Answer: B
Correct answer: B. A combination of qualitative and quantitative trends that enable decision making CISM RM 15th,: The degree of accuracy depends on how critical the measure is and varies considerably. Qualitative metrics may be approximations, but nevertheless may be adequate in many situations. Quantitative metrics need to be accurate to be of any use. Wrong answer: A. The number of key risk indicators (KRIs) identified, monitored, and acted upon CISM Q&A 9th, S1-6: Key risk indicators (KRIs) usually signal developing risk but do not indicate what the actual risk is. In that context, they are neither accurate nor reliable.
