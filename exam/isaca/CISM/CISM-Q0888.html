C. Maximum tolerable outage (MTO) The Maximum Tolerable Outage (MTO) is a key metric in a business continuity plan (BCP) that represents the maximum amount of time an organization can tolerate its operations being disrupted or unavailable before it incurs significant damage or loss. It is essentially the threshold beyond which the organization's ability to function is compromised to an unacceptable degree. Measuring the MTO helps organizations determine how long they need to maintain operations at an alternative site, such as a disaster recovery or backup location, to ensure business continuity during an outage or disaster scenario.
