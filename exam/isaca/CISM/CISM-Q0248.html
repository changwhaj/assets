Selected Answer: A
The most direct impact on the usability of an organization's asset classification policy is the granularity of classifications in the hierarchy. Asset classification is the process of identifying, categorizing, and labeling an organization's assets based on their value, criticality, and the risks associated with them. The granularity of classifications in the hierarchy refers to the level of detail and specificity in the classification scheme, and how finely assets are divided into different categories. A classification scheme with a high granularity will be more detailed and specific, making it easier to understand the different types of assets and their associated risks. This can improve the usability of the classification policy by making it easier for employees to understand which assets need to be protected and how they should be protected.
