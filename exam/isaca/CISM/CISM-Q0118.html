Selected Answer: A
The primary objective of presenting a business case for an information security initiative to senior management is to provide them with the necessary information to make an informed decision about whether or not to invest in the initiative. This includes information about the costs, benefits, and risks associated with the initiative, as well as an analysis of how the initiative aligns with the organization's overall business objectives. Representing stakeholders who will benefit from enhancements in information security (option B) is one of the important aspect when presenting the business case but not the primary reason. Providing management with the status of the information security program (option C) and demonstrating to management the due diligence involved with selecting the solution (option D) are also important aspects of presenting a business case, but they are not the primary reason.

B. Assess the business impact. Before implementing any security measures or deciding to transfer the risk to a third party, the information security manager should first assess the business impact of the increased residual risk. Understanding the potential impact of the risk is important in order to prioritize and plan mitigation activities. Once the business impact is assessed, the organization can then make informed decisions about which security measures to implement, or whether to transfer the risk to a third party. Finally, it is important to communicate the information to senior management to ensure they are aware of the increased residual risk and can make informed decisions about how to manage it.
