Selected Answer: D
D. Business process owners should be the first person the security manager should consult when determining the severity level of a security incident involving a third-party vendor. Business process owners have a good understanding of the criticality of the business process that the vendor supports and the impact of the security incident on the process. Risk manager, IT process owners and Business partners can also provide important information, but business process owners should be the first point of contact.

D. Business process owners The reason for consulting with business process owners first is that they have the most direct knowledge and understanding of the specific processes and systems impacted by the security incident. They can provide insights into the potential impact on the business operations and help assess the severity of the incident within the context of their processes. After consulting with business process owners, it is essential to engage with other relevant parties, such as IT process owners, risk managers, and business partners, to get a comprehensive view of the incident's severity and its potential consequences. However, the input from business process owners is crucial in the initial assessment.
