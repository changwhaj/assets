The option that BEST enables the design of an effective incident escalation process is: A. A well-defined organizational hierarchy. A well-defined organizational hierarchy provides clear lines of authority and responsibility within an organization. It establishes roles and reporting structures, which are crucial for incident escalation. In the event of an incident, a well-defined hierarchy ensures that there are designated individuals or teams responsible for receiving and escalating incident reports. This enables a structured and efficient process for escalating incidents to the appropriate levels of management and response teams.
