Selected Answer: B
When determining if an information asset has been classified appropriately, the most important consideration for an information security manager is to ensure alignment with security policy requirements. Security policies provide the framework and guidelines for classifying and protecting information assets within an organization. They define the criteria, rules, and standards for assigning appropriate classifications based on the sensitivity, criticality, value, or regulatory requirements of the information. By referring to the security policy requirements, the information security manager can assess whether the information asset has been classified correctly and in accordance with the established policies. This involves evaluating factors such as the level of sensitivity or confidentiality, integrity requirements, availability needs, and any specific regulatory or compliance obligations that apply to the asset.
