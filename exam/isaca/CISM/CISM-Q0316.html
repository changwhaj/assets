C. strengthens employee accountability. When security responsibilities are clearly outlined in job descriptions, employees are more likely to understand their specific roles and responsibilities related to security. This clarity helps ensure that employees are aware of their obligations when it comes to safeguarding sensitive information and following security protocols. It also makes it easier to hold employees accountable for security-related tasks and behaviors, as they are explicitly defined in their job descriptions. This, in turn, contributes to an overall culture of security within the organization. While the other options (A, B, and D) may also be important aspects of a comprehensive security strategy, strengthening employee accountability is the primary reason for embedding security responsibilities into job descriptions.

This helps establish a culture of security within the organization and ensures that employees understand their specific roles and responsibilities regarding security measures. It enhances their awareness of security expectations, promotes adherence to security policies and procedures, and fosters a sense of ownership and responsibility for safeguarding organizational assets.
