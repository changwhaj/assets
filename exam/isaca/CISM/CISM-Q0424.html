C. detection and response. When evaluating the risk from external hackers, the maximum exposure time would be the difference between detection and response. Detection refers to the process of identifying that a security incident has occurred, such as the presence of a hacker on a network. Response refers to the actions taken to contain and mitigate the incident, such as isolating the affected systems, removing the hacker from the network, and restoring normal operations. The maximum exposure time represents the duration of the incident from the moment the hacker is able to penetrate the network to the moment when the incident is fully resolved.
