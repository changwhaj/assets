Selected Answer: B
Once you've identified and analyzed the risks, it's important to prioritize them based on their potential impact and likelihood. This prioritization helps in deciding which risks should be addressed first and how to allocate resources for risk treatment. After prioritizing the risks, you can then proceed to identify controls for risk mitigation, identify the risk owner, and establish a plan to monitor the risks.
