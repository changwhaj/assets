A. adequate backup and restoration processes are in place. Preventing ransomware attacks requires a combination of different security controls, but having adequate backup and restoration processes in place is particularly important. Ransomware is a type of malware that encrypts the victim's files, making them inaccessible, and demands a ransom payment in exchange for the decryption key. Having a recent and tested backup of all important data can help the organization to recover from a ransomware attack without having to pay the ransom or losing any data. Additionally, regular security awareness training for employees can also help in preventing ransomware attacks. They should be trained on how to identify and avoid phishing attempts, malicious links, and suspicious attachments, which are often used to deliver ransomware payloads.
