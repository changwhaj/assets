The correct answer is D. IT security risk and exposure would be most helpful in determining an organization's current capacity to mitigate risks. IT security risk and exposure is a measure of the potential for loss or damage resulting from threats and vulnerabilities in an organization's IT environment. By assessing the likelihood and impact of potential risks and exposures, an organization can determine its current capacity to mitigate these risks and exposures. This information can be used to prioritize risk mitigation efforts and allocate resources accordingly. Capability maturity model (A) is a framework for evaluating and improving an organization's processes, while vulnerability assessment (B) is a method for identifying and quantifying vulnerabilities in an organization's IT environment. Business impact analysis (C) is a process for identifying an organization's critical business functions and the impact of disruptions to those functions.
