D. Role-based access control (RBAC). Role-based access control is a security strategy that limits access to computer systems and data based on individuals' roles or job functions within an organization. It ensures that individuals only have access to the information and resources necessary for them to perform their job duties, and nothing more. This approach minimizes the potential for unauthorized access to sensitive data by limiting access privileges to only what is required for an individual's specific role. While all the options listed (regular audits of access controls, strong background checks when hiring staff, and prompt termination procedures) are important components of an overall security strategy, RBAC directly addresses the issue of restricting and controlling access to confidential information, making it the most effective defense against malicious insiders. It's worth noting that a comprehensive security strategy often involves a combination of these measures to provide layered defense against insider threats, but RBAC is a fundamental and effective component of that strategy.
