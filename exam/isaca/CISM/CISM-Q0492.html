A. Business strategy. While regulatory requirements, information security strategy, and data classification are important considerations for information security, they should all align with and support the broader goals and objectives of the organization's business strategy. Information security should be seen as an enabler of the business strategy rather than a standalone goal. By aligning information security objectives with the business strategy, an organization can ensure that its security efforts are focused on protecting the most critical assets and achieving the overall goals of the business.

A. Business strategy should be the PRIMARY basis for determining information security objectives. Determining information security objectives based on business strategy will help ensure that the organization's resources are allocated in a way that maximizes the protection of the organization's assets while supporting the organization's overall mission and objectives.
