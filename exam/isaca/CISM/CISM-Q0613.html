The question stated "security program" not governance C. Risk appetite While all the options listed (regulatory requirements, business strategy, and available resources) are important factors in shaping a security program, the primary driver should be the organization's risk appetite. Risk appetite defines the level of risk an organization is willing to accept to achieve its objectives. The security program should align with this risk appetite to protect the organization's assets and data while allowing it to operate effectively and efficiently. Regulatory requirements, business strategy, and available resources should all be considered within the context of managing and mitigating the organization's specific risk landscape.
