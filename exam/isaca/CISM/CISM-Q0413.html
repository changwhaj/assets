C. Business impact analysis (BIA) should contain the INITIAL prioritization of recovery of services. A business impact analysis is a key component of business continuity planning, which involves identifying critical business processes and the resources required to support them. By conducting a BIA, an organization can identify the potential impacts of a disruption to its business operations and prioritize the recovery of services based on the criticality of the processes and resources required to support them.

Selected Answer: C
The initial prioritization of recovery of services should be contained in a Business Impact Analysis (BIA). A BIA is a document that assesses the potential consequences of an interruption to critical business operations and services, and it is used to determine the priority of those services in the event of a disaster. The BIA considers factors such as the criticality of business functions, the dependencies between different business processes, and the consequences of a service interruption, and it prioritizes the recovery of services based on these factors
