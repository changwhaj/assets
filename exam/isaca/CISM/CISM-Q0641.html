Selected Answer: B
The correct answer is (B) Risk assessment as the risk assessment takes the assets, threat, impact, probability, and context in mind when giving the result. It also provides guidance for how decisions should be made. Rationale: (A) Business impact analysis (BIA) is incorrect cause although it does have the impact and assets it does not include the threat or probability of something occurring. Additionally, it can be used within a risk assessment, but a risk assessment can't be used within a BIA. (C) Vulnerability assessment says what the risks are, but not the likelihood. So it would be hard to prioritize based on vulnerabilities alone. (D) Industry best practices are great, but all the things in the industry may not be relevant to your organization. Or it may miss things for your org. So it is not ideal.

Selected Answer: A
A risk assessment is the most helpful for determining which information security policies should be implemented by an organization because it provides an understanding of the organization's current risk posture. The risk assessment process identifies potential threats, assesses the likelihood of those threats occurring, and evaluates the impact of those threats on the organization's critical assets. This information is then used to prioritize the risks and determine the most appropriate risk treatment options. The risk assessment results can then be used to inform the development of information security policies that align with the organization's risk management strategy and address the most critical security risks. By focusing on the risks that pose the greatest threat to the organization, a risk assessment helps organizations to make informed decisions about which security policies to implement and how to allocate their resources effectively to improve their overall security posture.
