A. An accurate and complete information asset catalog is the best indication that information assets are classified accurately. An information asset catalog is a comprehensive inventory of an organization's information assets, which includes details such as their location, classification, sensitivity, and ownership. It serves as a central repository of information about all the assets an organization possesses and helps to ensure that assets are properly identified, classified, and managed.
