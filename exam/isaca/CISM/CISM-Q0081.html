Answer is A Including information security clauses within contracts is the most effective way for an organization to ensure its third-party service providers are aware of information security requirements and expectations. By explicitly outlining information security requirements and expectations within contracts, organizations establish clear guidelines and obligations for third-party providers to adhere to. These clauses may cover aspects such as data protection, confidentiality, access controls, incident response procedures, and compliance with relevant regulations. While auditing the service delivery of third-party providers (Option B), providing information security training to third-party personnel (Option C), and requiring third parties to sign confidentiality agreements (Option D) are all important measures, including information security clauses within contracts establishes a formal agreement and legal framework that explicitly addresses information security concerns and responsibilities.
