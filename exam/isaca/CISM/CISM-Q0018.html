Answer should be A. Alot of people are saying D but Clcomplying with regulatory requirements is also important, but it should not be the primary focus. Regulatory requirements provide a minimum level of security that must be met, but they do not necessarily ensure that the system is adequately protected. Vulnerabilities or threats by themselves are just half the story. Both must be considered together to understand risk (Risk = Threat x Vulnerability), making B and C void. While the amount of residual risk should be within acceptable limits, an overwhelming amount of risk in the context of highly confidential data is ‘regulatory’. In the case of highly confidential data like PII, PHI, financial data, trade secrets etc., countries and industries have varying regulations and stringent legal requirements (such as GDPR, PCI DSS). Litigations, class action, legal fines, regulatory sanctions, loss of reputation, loss of customer confidence are some major risks in this context. Therefore, regulatory requirements and compliance take precedence over all other considerations. Answer is D.
