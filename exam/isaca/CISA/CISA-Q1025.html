Selected Answer: B
In the context of ensuring the resiliency of business-critical applications migrated to the cloud, option B, "Negotiating a service level agreement (SLA) with the provider," is typically the MOST important. While all the options listed are important for application resiliency, negotiating a comprehensive SLA with the cloud provider helps establish clear expectations regarding uptime, performance, and other critical aspects of service. A well-defined SLA can provide legal guarantees and penalties for the provider if service levels are not met, which is essential for ensuring the reliability and resiliency of your applications. It serves as a contractual commitment to maintaining the required levels of availability and performance for your applications in the cloud.
