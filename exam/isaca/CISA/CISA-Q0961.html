An IS auditor has been asked to review a recently implemented quality management system (QMS). Which of the following should be the auditor’s PRIMARY focus?

  • A. Training materials prepared for coaching employees
  • B. Processes to measure the performance of business-critical transactions
  • C. Cost-benefit analysis of the development and implementation of the QMS
  • D. Stability of the implemented QMS system over a period of time

The PRIMARY focus of an IS (Information Systems) auditor when reviewing a recently implemented quality management system (QMS) should be on B. Processes to measure the performance of business-critical transactions. While all the options listed are important aspects to consider when auditing a QMS, ensuring that the processes to measure the performance of business-critical transactions are working effectively is essential. This is because the primary goal of a QMS is to ensure that the organization's processes are efficient, effective, and consistently meeting quality standards, especially for critical business operations. Therefore, reviewing and validating the performance measurement processes related to these critical transactions is crucial to determine whether the QMS is achieving its intended objectives.
