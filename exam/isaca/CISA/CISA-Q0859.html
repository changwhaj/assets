Selected Answer: B
Data backups (B): Data is usually the most critical asset for an organization. Therefore, restoring data backups is often the first step in disaster recovery. This ensures that important data is available for use as soon as possible. Operating system (A): Once data is restored, the next priority is to bring up the operating system. The operating system is necessary to run applications and provide a platform for other services. Applications (D): After the operating system is in place, applications can be restored. This includes essential business applications that the organization relies on to function. Decision support system (C): Decision support systems, while important, are typically of lower priority compared to data, operating systems, and essential applications. They can be restored after the core infrastructure and critical applications are up and running.
