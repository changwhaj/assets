Selected Answer: D
D. The auditor has a personal relationship with an end user. Auditor independence is a critical aspect of the auditing process to ensure that auditors can provide unbiased and objective opinions about the financial statements and other matters they are auditing. In this context, a personal relationship between the auditor and an end user (client personnel) can raise concerns about independence because it may compromise the auditor's ability to remain impartial and objective when evaluating the client's financial information. Such a relationship can create the perception that the auditor may not be able to provide an unbiased assessment of the client's financial statements or internal controls. Therefore, option D is the scenario that should raise a concern about auditor independence.
