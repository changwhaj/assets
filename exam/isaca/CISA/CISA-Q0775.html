Selected Answer: A
In a role-based environment with an IT segregation of duties policy, an application developer (Option A) may be assigned the role of emergency support. This involves providing support and troubleshooting assistance during unexpected outages or other incidents that impact the availability of the IT systems. The role of emergency support typically requires a good understanding of the application and the underlying infrastructure, which an application developer is likely to have. The roles of system administration (Option B), IT operator (Option C), and database administration (Option D) typically involve tasks such as configuring, maintaining, and monitoring IT systems, and may require access to sensitive information or critical systems. These roles are typically separated from the role of application development to ensure that the segregation of duties policy is maintained and that critical IT functions are performed by different individuals.
