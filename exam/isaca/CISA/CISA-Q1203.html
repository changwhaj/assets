Selected Answer: B
B. Access authorization tables can best achieve effective separation of duties in an online environment. Access authorization tables are typically used in system security to control and manage user access rights and privileges. By properly configuring access authorization tables, organizations can assign different levels of access to individuals based on their job roles and responsibilities. This helps enforce separation of duties by ensuring that individuals only have access to the systems and data necessary for performing their specific tasks, reducing the risk of unauthorized actions or errors. While appropriate supervision, transaction logging, and written procedure manuals are also valuable controls, access authorization tables specifically focus on managing and enforcing separation of duties in online environments.
