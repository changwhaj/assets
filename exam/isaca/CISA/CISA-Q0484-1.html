B: Independence is a fundamental principle of IS auditing and requires that the auditor remain impartial and free from any conflicts of interest. The auditor must be able to exercise professional judgment without being influenced by any personal or financial interest in the outcome of the audit.

Several attributes related to auditors’ integrity: Integrity implies honesty, fair dealing, and truthfulness in professional judgments, respect for the policies and decisions, and is unbiased and free of conflicts of interest. Working experiences at the previous firm can lead to biased judgment. By default, it cannot be considered an independence violation. If a member or his or her firm had any cooperative agreement with the client that was material to the member's firm or the client during the term of a professional engagement, the member's or firm's independence would be impaired. So, option C is the correct answer.
