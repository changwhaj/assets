Selected Answer: A
The option that should be of greatest concern to an IS auditor reviewing an organization's business continuity plan (BCP) is A. The BCP has not been tested since it was first issued. The purpose of a BCP is to ensure that the organization can continue to operate in the event of a crisis or disruption. The effectiveness of the BCP cannot be determined unless it has been tested, and this testing is essential for identifying any weaknesses or gaps in the plan. The BCP must be able to respond quickly and effectively to minimize any damage and reduce the impact of the disruption on the business. If the BCP has not been tested since it was first issued, the IS auditor may not have confidence in the plan's ability to handle an actual crisis, and this could put the organization at risk.
