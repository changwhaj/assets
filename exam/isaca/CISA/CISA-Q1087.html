Selected Answer: A
Detective Control: This is a type of control designed to identify and report errors or irregularities after they have occurred. Key verification falls into this category because it detects data entry errors after the initial entry by comparing it against a second entry. Compensating Control: A compensating control is an alternative control put in place to mitigate the risk when the primary control is deemed inadequate or is not feasible. It's essentially a backup measure, used when the preferred method of controlling a risk is too costly or complex. Compensating controls are generally used to achieve the same control objective as the primary control.

Selected Answer: A
Key verification is a form of detective control. It is designed to detect errors or discrepancies in data entry after the initial entry has occurred. By having data entered twice, usually by two different individuals, and then comparing the two sets of data, discrepancies can be identified and corrected. This process happens after the initial data entry error occurs, hence it is a detective control, not a preventive one.

Selected Answer: A
The bank has implemented a Detective control. A Detective control is designed to discover or detect errors or discrepancies after they have occurred. In this case, the system identifies mismatches between the first and second input of monetary amounts on check images, serving as a detective control to catch discrepancies. Corrective controls are implemented to correct or remedy an issue after it has been identified, while Compensating controls offset the lack of effectiveness in other controls. Deterrent controls are implemented to discourage individuals from violating policies or procedures, often through the threat of penalties.
