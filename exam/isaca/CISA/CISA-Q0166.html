The most significant risk associated with the use of virtualization is a single point of failure. Virtualization involves consolidating multiple virtual machines (VMs) onto a single physical server or host. If the host experiences a failure or outage, it can potentially impact multiple VMs running on that host, leading to a significant disruption of services.
