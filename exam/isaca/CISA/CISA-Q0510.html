Performance data is considered the best source of information for assessing the effectiveness of IT process monitoring because it provides objective and quantifiable evidence of the efficiency and effectiveness of the processes being monitored. Performance data can include metrics such as processing times, resource utilization, error rates, and more. By analyzing performance data, IT teams can identify areas where processes can be improved, determine if IT resources are being used optimally, and make data-driven decisions about process monitoring and improvement. In contrast, participative management techniques, quality assurance (QA) reviews, and real-time audit software may provide useful information, but performance data is the most direct and tangible measure of process effectiveness.
