Selected Answer: C
C. The media involved is preserved using imaging, and further analysis is performed on the image instead of the original. Preserving the original media through imaging is a fundamental practice in computer forensics. It ensures the integrity of the evidence by creating a bit-for-bit copy of the original media. This copy (the forensic image) can be analyzed and investigated without altering or damaging the original evidence. This approach helps maintain the chain of custody and ensures that the evidence is legally admissible in court because it proves the integrity and authenticity of the data collected during the investigation. Option A is not a good approach because securely deleting evidence can lead to the loss of crucial information and may not be legally acceptable. Option B is a reasonable practice but does not emphasize the importance of preserving the original media through imaging. Option D is also not a good approach because isolating the computer without proper imaging and evidence preservation can result in potential data loss or contamination.
