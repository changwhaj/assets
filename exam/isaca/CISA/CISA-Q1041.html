Option C. According to the Certified Information Systems Auditor (CISA) exam, the MOST important thing to define within a disaster recovery plan (DRP) is option C: A comprehensive list of disaster recovery scenarios and priorities. A comprehensive list of disaster recovery scenarios and priorities is critical in a disaster recovery plan (DRP) as it helps ensure that the organization is prepared to respond to a range of potential disasters effectively. The list should identify all the possible disaster scenarios that could affect the organization and their relative priorities. The list should also identify the critical systems, applications, and data that must be recovered first in the event of a disaster.

Selected Answer: C
While all the options are important components of a disaster recovery plan (DRP), defining a comprehensive list of disaster recovery scenarios and priorities is MOST important. This involves identifying potential disasters, disruptions, or incidents that could affect the organization and prioritizing them based on their impact and criticality. Understanding the potential scenarios allows the organization to develop targeted and effective strategies for recovery. It helps in prioritizing resources, defining response procedures, and ensuring that critical systems and processes are restored in a manner that aligns with the organization's priorities and business needs. Roles and responsibilities, test results, and integration with the business continuity plan (BCP) are also crucial elements, but the foundation of a DRP lies in a thorough understanding of potential disaster scenarios and the priorities associated with them.
