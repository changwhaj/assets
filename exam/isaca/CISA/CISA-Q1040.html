Decommissioning old tapes is the best feature among the options for realizing cost savings in backup management practices. This process involves removing outdated or redundant backup tapes that are no longer needed, thus reducing storage costs and streamlining backup operations. Refreshing data, rotating tapes, and defragmenting data on tapes are important aspects of backup management but are not directly related to cost savings in the same way that decommissioning old tapes is.
