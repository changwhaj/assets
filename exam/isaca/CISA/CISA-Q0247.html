The FIRST priority of an information security manager after a high-profile system has been compromised is to preserve incident-related data. This is because the incident data can help in investigating the cause and extent of the compromise, as well as in identifying any data that may have been lost or stolen. Failure to preserve the incident data may result in loss of critical evidence and hinder any further investigation. Once the incident-related data has been preserved, the information security manager can then proceed with identifying the malware that caused the compromise, restoring the compromised system, and implementing improvements to prevent recurrence.

An information security manager's first priority after a high-profile system has been compromised is to preserve incident-related data. This includes collecting and preserving any data or evidence that may be relevant to the incident, such as log files, network traffic, system images, and other relevant data. This step is crucial in order to conduct a thorough investigation of the incident, understand the cause and extent of the compromise, and help identify any potential suspects. Once the incident-related data is preserved, the next step would be to identify the malware that compromised the system, this will help to understand the type of attack and the malware used.
