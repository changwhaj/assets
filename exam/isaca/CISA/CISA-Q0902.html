Project sponsors Project sponsors would provide the IS (Information Systems) auditor with the MOST helpful input during an interview to determine whether business requirements for an application were met. Project sponsors are typically individuals or groups within an organization who initiate and fund projects, including application development projects. They are often the ones who define the initial business requirements and objectives for the application. As such, they have a deep understanding of the intended goals and outcomes of the project. Therefore, their input is critical for assessing whether those business requirements have been met and if the project has delivered the expected value to the organization.
