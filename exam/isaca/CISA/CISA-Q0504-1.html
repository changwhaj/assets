  • D. develop a business case

Selected Answer: C
From the point of view of the InfoSec Manager, the main concern will be the security risks associated with such decision. Thus the Info Sec manager should conduct a risk assessment to this effect. The mapping of strategy to business objectives, development of business case and cost-benefit analysis should be performed by the Senior Management, NOT the InfoSec Manager

BlackGarlic
2022-03-26 03:46
Option A: Cost-benefit analysis is part of a business case analysis. Option B: A strategy map is "knowledge-oriented," with strategic improvement topics like "growth" or "productivity" at the forefront. The strategy map defines a critical improvement objective and attempts to gain management support and promote participation in the internal business planning at all levels. Option C: Risk assessment is useful to predict the likelihood of highly impactful events and countermeasures. Option D: A business case is "problem-oriented" and explores all feasible approaches to a given solution, allowing decision-makers to select the best option based on benefits and drawbacks. Given that we have one considered improvement strategy, the best answer is option B.
