C. Expected time to resolve incidents is not specified. The expected time to resolve incidents is a critical aspect of incident management. It helps establish clear service level agreements (SLAs) and expectations regarding incident resolution times. Without specified resolution timeframes, incidents may linger unresolved for extended periods, leading to potential disruptions, security breaches, and dissatisfied stakeholders. This lack of clarity can undermine the effectiveness of incident management processes and compromise the organization's security and operational stability.
