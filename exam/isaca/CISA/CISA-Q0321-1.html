Which of the following is the PRIMARY reason for an IS auditor to map out the narrative of a business process?

  • A. To verify the business process is as described in the engagement letter
  • B. To identify the resources required to perform the audit
  • C. To ensure alignment with organizational objectives
  • D. To gain insight into potential risks

From CRM 27th, A risk-based audit approach focuses on the understanding of the nature of the business and being able to identify and categorize risk. Business risk impacts the long-term viability of a specific business. Thus, an IS auditor using a risk-based audit approach must be able to understand business processes. The answer is D. To gain insight into potential risks

Based on audit planning priorities, IS auditors define their starting point by identifying their objective state and determining the methods and resources that will get them there using a strategy. Therefore, the proposed strategy should reflect how information systems, technologies relevant to business activities and processes fit in with the overall risk-based approach to auditing and how many resources are required to perform the audit. In other words, it's not about gaining insight into the risks; it's about determining the dimensions of the business process elements that contribute to business risk, as well as how many resources we'll need to do an appropriate audit on those elements. So, option B is the correct answer
