Selected Answer: B
Role-based access controls (RBAC) are a widely recognized and effective approach to managing access to sensitive data. RBAC ensures that individuals are granted access rights based on their roles and responsibilities within the organization. This means that only authorized personnel who require access to confidential data for their job functions will have permission to view or manipulate it. By implementing RBAC, organizations can enforce the principle of least privilege and reduce the risk of data leakage to unauthorized stakeholders.

Selected Answer: A
A. Create a data classification policy. Creating a data classification policy is the most effective way to mitigate the risk of confidential data leakage to unauthorized stakeholders. This policy should clearly define the different levels of data sensitivity and the appropriate controls that should be in place to protect each level. This ensures that data is handled and protected according to its level of sensitivity, and that only authorized personnel have access to it. Implementing role-based access controls, requiring the use of login credentials and passwords and conducting information security awareness training are all important measures to protect data, but creating a data classification policy is the most effective as it addresses the risk of data leakage from the root cause.
