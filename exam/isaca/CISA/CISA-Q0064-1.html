A is correct. If you normalize too much, a simple concept can end up having 100 arms spread out all over the database. Then it's more work and has very little benefit, aside from longer queries, some performance issues and bragging about your super normalized database. B) nope C) reduced data redundancy - nope because this is a benefit of normalization D) decreased performance - nope because you will have 1) more performance at first, 2) if you have long queries, you still have A) enhanced complexity as a higher risk.
