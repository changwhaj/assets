Selected Answer: C
The organization's risk tolerance has the greatest influence on an organization's information security strategy. Risk tolerance is the level of risk an organization is willing to accept or tolerate in pursuit of its objectives. An organization's information security strategy should be designed to manage risks within its risk tolerance. Industry security standards, organizational structure, and information security awareness can also influence an organization's information security strategy, but risk tolerance is the most significant factor.

Risk tolerance refers to the level of risk that an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its business objectives. This can be influenced by a variety of factors, such as the organization's culture, industry, regulatory environment, and business goals. C An organization's risk tolerance is a key factor in developing its information security strategy because it determines the level of investment that the organization is willing to make in security controls and the types of controls that are implemented. For example, an organization with a low risk tolerance may choose to invest heavily in security controls to reduce the risk of security incidents, while an organization with a high risk tolerance may choose to accept a greater level of risk and invest less in security controls.
