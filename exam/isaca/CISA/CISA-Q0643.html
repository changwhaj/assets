the InfoSec policy should be reviewed to ensure its alignment with the results of the IT risk assessment. All other options do not necessarily indicate a need for InfoSec policy review as the policy may already cover these scenarios. Policies need to be updated to reflect new technology, changes in environment (e.g., regulatory compliance requirements), and significant changes in business processes in exploiting IT for efficiency and effectiveness in productivity or competitive gains. All of these would be covered in the result of the risk assessment

Option B: In any situation, the complexity term is one of the most basic aspects that has a direct impact on risk. Therefore, any increase in the complexity of business operations or data flows can have a significant change in the security posture, which makes the need for policy review a necessity. Option C: Security exceptions are made when a condition does not align with formal security expectations as defined by policy, standard, and/or procedure. IT Systems must have valid reasons for not meeting one or more of these standards. We apply exception requests to help reduce risk when these situations occur. Security exceptions are only approved when compensating controls are in place and do not make significant changes to existing systems, infrastructure, or services. They are also typically temporary in nature and existence, which eliminates the necessity for policy revision. So, Option B is the correct answer.
