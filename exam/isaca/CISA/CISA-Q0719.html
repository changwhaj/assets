  • A. Users have not been trained on the new system.
  • B. The new system is not platform agnostic.
  • C. Data from the legacy system is not migrated correctly to the new system.
  • D. The new system has resulted in layoffs of key experienced personnel.

When reviewing the deployment of a new automated system, the most significant risk among the given options would depend on the specific circumstances and the organization's priorities. However, considering the potential impact on business operations and continuity, as well as the potential for financial and reputational damage, the finding that presents the MOST significant risk would likely be: C. Data from the legacy system is not migrated correctly to the new system. This finding poses several risks. Incorrect migration of data can lead to data loss, data corruption, or data inconsistencies, which can significantly impact business operations, decision-making, and customer satisfaction. It can result in inaccurate financial reporting, legal compliance issues, and a loss of trust from stakeholders. Additionally, rectifying data migration issues can be complex, time-consuming, and expensive.
