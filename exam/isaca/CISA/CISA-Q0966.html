The PRIMARY reason for an IS (Information Systems) auditor to recommend using key risk indicators (KRIs) is: C. To determine whether risk is changing. Key risk indicators (KRIs) are metrics or data points that provide early warning signs of potential changes in risk. They help organizations monitor and assess the evolving risk landscape. By using KRIs, IS auditors can identify shifts in risk levels and take proactive measures to mitigate those risks before they escalate. KRIs provide a way to monitor and track risk in real-time or at regular intervals, making it easier to assess whether risk is increasing, decreasing, or remaining stable. While the other options (A, B, and D) are important considerations in risk management and auditing, the primary purpose of KRIs is to provide insight into the changing nature of risks, allowing organizations to respond effectively.
