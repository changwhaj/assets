The MOST appropriate person to chair the steering committee for an enterprise-wide system development is: D. executive level manager The steering committee is typically responsible for making high-level decisions, setting priorities, and providing overall direction for a project of this scale. An executive-level manager, such as a Chief Information Officer (CIO) or Chief Technology Officer (CTO), is well-suited to chair the committee because they have the authority and strategic perspective necessary to align the project with the organization's goals and objectives. While business analysts, project managers, and IS directors may play important roles in the development process, the chair of the steering committee should have a broader organizational perspective and decision-making authority.
