  • B. The organization does not have a social media policy.
  • C. Employees are using corporate devices to access mainstream social media websites.
  • D. Employees are using corporate branding on personal social media postings.

When performing an audit of an organization's social media practices, the IS auditor should prioritize concerns that pose the greatest risks to the organization's security, reputation, and compliance. D. Employees are using corporate branding on personal social media postings. This is of the GREATEST concern to an IS auditor in the context of social media practices audit. The use of corporate branding on personal social media postings can potentially lead to reputation risks, confusion among customers or partners, and even legal issues if employees misrepresent the organization. It's crucial to ensure that corporate branding is used appropriately and in accordance with the organization's branding guidelines.
